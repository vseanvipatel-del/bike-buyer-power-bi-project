# bike-buyer-power-bi-project
# 🚲 Bike Buyer Dashboard Report & Performance Analysis

An end-to-end Data Analysis and Business Intelligence project exploring consumer demographics, purchase behaviors, and micro-mobility trends. This project transforms transactional customer logs from the `Bike Buyer Dashboard.xlsx` spreadsheet into structured data models, paired with executive insights designed to optimize retail targeting and marketing conversions[cite: 15, 16].

## 📌 Project Overview
The objective of this project is to analyze the underlying factors that determine whether a customer will purchase a bicycle[cite: 16]. By evaluating demographics such as income, commute distance, gender, and age, this analysis establishes clear customer personas and outlines actionable strategies for premium product positioning and targeted digital advertising[cite: 16].

---

## 📊 Core Business Insights & Visual Trends

The analysis evaluates 1,026 total purchasers to uncover key retail performance metrics[cite: 16]:

### 👥 1. Market Share & Demographic Breakdown
* **Gender-Neutral Equilibrium:** The customer base is exceptionally well-balanced, consisting of **525 Male purchasers (51%)** and **501 Female purchasers (49%)**[cite: 16]. This distribution suggests that gender-neutral marketing frameworks yield the most efficient conversion rates[cite: 16].
* **Age Peak Performance:** The primary demographic sweet spot is centered heavily within the **40-year-old age bin**, generating **185 sales at peak** and serving as the highest conversion threshold for active buyers[cite: 16].

### 💰 2. Financial & Income Correlations
* **Affluence & Conversion:** Across all genders, an increase in average income directly correlates with a successful bike purchase[cite: 16]. 
* **Income Tiers:** Male purchasers represent a higher premium bracket with a **$60k average income**, while Female purchasers average a **$55k income tier**[cite: 16].

### 🗺️ 3. Commute Proximity & Micro-Mobility Sensitivity
* **Short-Range Sweet Spot:** Proximity to work is the strongest geographic predictor of sales, with maximum customer engagement occurring in the **0-1 mile commute range (171 successful purchases)**[cite: 16].
* **Long-Distance Decline:** Interest experiences a sharp decline for customers facing 10+ mile commutes, demonstrating that bicycles are predominantly viewed as a solution for short-range micro-mobility or first/last-mile transit[cite: 16].

---

## 🛠️ Actionable Business Strategies Derived

Based on the visual data trends, the repository outlines three core strategic initiatives[cite: 16]:
1. **Targeted Campaigns:** Direct high-budget digital campaigns heavily toward the 35–45 age bracket to capitalize on the highest converting age threshold[cite: 16].
2. **Micro-Mobility Positioning:** Position and market bicycles specifically as optimized solutions for short-range urban transit and first/last-mile micro-mobility[cite: 16].
3. **Premium Slicing:** Introduce premium model tiers aimed at high-earning male consumers and tailored accessories tailored for the female buyer segment[cite: 16].
